How Our Technology Works

A sophisticated multi-step process for accurate autism detection

1

Image Acquisition

Users upload facial photographs through our secure platform. The system accepts various image formats and automatically optimizes them for analysis.

2

Facial Landmark Detection

Our AI identifies key facial landmarks including eyes, nose, mouth, and facial contours to establish a baseline for expression analysis.

3

Expression Analysis

The system analyzes micro-expressions, gaze patterns, and facial muscle movements that research has linked to autism spectrum disorder.

4

AI Classification

Our MobileNet model processes the extracted features and classifies the image with a confidence score indicating likelihood of autism spectrum disorder.

Frequently Asked Questions

Common questions about our AI-powered autism detection system

How accurate is your autism detection system?
Our MobileNet model achieves 81.67% accuracy in clinical validation studies, outperforming traditional screening methods. We continuously work to improve our models through ongoing research and validation.
Is this system meant to replace clinical diagnosis?
No, our system is designed as a screening tool to identify potential cases that should receive further clinical evaluation. It augments but does not replace professional medical diagnosis by qualified healthcare providers.
What age range is this system appropriate for?
Our current models are optimized for children aged 2-8 years, which is the critical window for early autism detection and intervention. We are expanding our research to include broader age ranges.
How is user privacy protected?
We employ end-to-end encryption, strict data anonymization protocols, and comply with healthcare privacy regulations including HIPAA. User data is never shared with third parties without explicit consent.
What types of images work best for analysis?
Clear, well-lit front-facing photographs with neutral expressions yield the most accurate results. The system can process various image formats but works best with high-resolution images where facial features are clearly visible.
