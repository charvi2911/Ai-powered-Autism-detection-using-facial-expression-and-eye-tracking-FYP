Understanding Autism Spectrum Disorder (ASD)

Autism Spectrum Disorder (ASD) is a neurodevelopmental condition characterized by challenges with social communication, social interaction, and restricted or repetitive behaviors. The term "spectrum" reflects the wide variation in challenges and strengths possessed by each person with autism.

Common characteristics may include:

  • Difficulties with social communication and interaction
  • Restricted or repetitive behaviors and interests
  • Sensory sensitivities
  • Different ways of learning, moving, or paying attention

Understanding Typically Developing (TD)

Typically Developing (TD) refers to individuals who are following the expected patterns of development in areas such as social skills, communication, behavior, and cognitive abilities.

Key aspects of typical development include:

  • Age-appropriate social communication skills
  • Flexible thinking and behavior patterns
  • Expected developmental milestones in language, social, and cognitive domains
  • Typical sensory processing and responses

Note: This screening result suggests development is progressing as expected. If you have any concerns about development, consulting with a professional is always recommended.
